# ISEF Judge Skill — Major Updates from 2024 Internal Training Materials

## Overview

This document summarizes the critical insider insights from internal ISEF judge training materials (Dallas 2024) that have been incorporated into the science-fair-judge skill. These are the "secret" aspects of ISEF judging that are not publicly documented but are essential for accurate evaluation.

## Critical Discoveries

### 1. **Scores Are NOT the Final Answer**

**What we learned:**
- Judges submit a SINGLE 0-100 integer score (not per-criterion scores)
- Scores undergo two-stage normalization to eliminate judge bias
- However, **actual awards are determined through caucus discussion**, not scores alone
- ~5% of judges never score below 80, ~5% never score above 80
- The caucus process is where real decisions happen

**Impact on skill:**
- Updated `isef-rubric.md` with detailed explanation of caucus process
- Added two-phase caucus explanation (Growing the Pool → Selecting Awardees)
- Added three-table system (Awarded/Pool/Rejected)
- Emphasized that interview performance affects both score AND advocacy potential
- Added "Caucus Advocacy Assessment" to report template

### 2. **Interview Time Reality**

**What we learned:**
- 15-minute slots, but only 10-12 minutes of actual conversation
- Transition time between judges reduces available time
- Students who monopolize with rehearsed presentations prevent judges from asking probing questions
- Best approach: 1-2 minute overview, then let judge drive

**Impact on skill:**
- Corrected interview duration from "15 minutes" to "10-12 minutes actual conversation"
- Added critical warning against rehearsed presentations
- Added interview control techniques for judges
- Added "apparently futile interview" scenarios

### 3. **The "My5 Projects" System**

**What we learned:**
- Judges select their 5 favorite projects for additional interviews in periods 19-20
- This is CRUCIAL for award determination
- Allows judges to revisit borderline projects
- Judges can only select projects they've already interviewed
- Selections must be made by end of Period 18

**Impact on skill:**
- Added "My5 Projects" section to `isef-rubric.md`
- Explained how this system affects award determination
- Added guidance on making a project memorable for judges

### 4. **Corrected Award Amounts**

## Key Insights for Students

Based on the internal training materials, here are the most important things for students to know:

1. **The interview is everything** — 25% of score, plus determines whether judges will advocate for you in caucus
2. **Don't give a rehearsed presentation** — spend 1-2 minutes on overview, then let judges ask questions
3. **Be ready for "What would you do differently?"** — this is THE most important question
4. **Show you did the work** — judges will test this aggressively with questions about difficulties, choices, and struggles
5. **Understand your limitations** — discussing what went wrong is the strongest signal of scientific maturity
6. **Make yourself memorable** — judges need material to advocate for you during caucus
7. **Get selected for "My5"** — additional interviews can make the difference between Pool and Rejected

---

## Key Insights for Judges

Based on the internal training materials, here are the most important things for judges to know:

1. **Scores are just input** — caucus discussion is where actual decisions happen
2. **Maintain interview control** — don't let students monopolize with rehearsed presentations
3. **Test for independence** — aggressively probe whether the student did the work themselves
4. **Be aware of bias** — use standardized questions and focus on scientific understanding over presentation
5. **No feedback to students** — never give hints about scores or awards
6. **Document concerns privately** — handle misconduct through proper channels, not directly with students
7. **Advocate based on merit** — during caucus, focus on project quality, not student demographics
