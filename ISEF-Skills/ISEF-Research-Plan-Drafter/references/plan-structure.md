# ISEF Research Plan — Required Structure

**Source:** `/Volumes/Mac-Mini/workspaces/tian2-edu/Competitions/isef-research-playbook/05-analysis/project-workflow-and-category-map-2026-05-01.md` Stage 1, citing ISEF Book.pdf lines 230–267 verbatim.

Per ISEF Book.pdf §General Requirements:

> "Project documentation should begin with the development of a research plan to detail the
> rationale, research question(s), materials, methodology and procedures, risk assessment and
> a bibliography for the proposed research." (Book.pdf 230–238)
>
> "This research plan should be completed before experimentation and will inform the forms
> required for your research." (Book.pdf 240–244)

The Research Plan / Project Summary accompanies Form 1A (Student Checklist) and is reviewed by
the SRC (or IRB for human-participant projects) before experimentation begins.

## Required sections

### A. Rationale

The rationale states what scientific problem or need the project addresses, why this is important,
and how the proposed work advances understanding or contributes to a solution. Should be 1-3
paragraphs depending on depth.

Strong rationale examples cite specific gaps in current literature; weak rationale generalizes
("X is an important problem" without specifying what is unknown).

### B. Research Questions, Hypotheses, Engineering Goals

For science projects: a testable hypothesis stated as a measurable prediction.

For engineering projects: design criteria, constraints, and definition of success.

The hypothesis must satisfy the same falsifiability standard as the ISEF Topic Finder's
hypothesis gate: state what result would prove the hypothesis wrong.

### C. Description of Method / Procedures

Step-by-step description of:
- Experimental design (independent / dependent variables; controls)
- Materials and equipment with specifications
- Sample size and recruitment / selection criteria
- Procedural details sufficient for another student to reproduce
- Modifications planned at decision points

For engineering: design iteration plan; testing protocol; performance criteria.

### D. Risk Assessment

Identifies hazards across all relevant substrate clusters:
- Human participants: privacy, psychological harm, physical safety, consent process
- Vertebrate animals: husbandry, humane endpoints, euthanasia method (if any)
- Tissue/blood/PHBA: biosafety level, sharps protocol, disposal
- Hazardous chemicals/devices: SDS-linked hazards, PPE, ventilation, disposal
- Field: terrain, weather, wildlife, lone-worker policy

Each identified hazard requires a mitigation. "No risks" is rarely acceptable — even
computational projects have intellectual-property and dual-use risk worth acknowledging.

### E. Data Analysis

States BEFORE data collection:
- How data will be analyzed
- Which statistical tests are appropriate for the design
- How outliers will be handled
- How missing data will be treated
- Pre-registered analysis plan vs. exploratory analyses (be honest about which is which)

A common SRC concern: post-hoc analysis fishing. Pre-specified analysis plans demonstrate rigor.

### F. Bibliography

Minimum 5 cited sources. Mix of:
- Primary research (peer-reviewed journal articles) — at least 3
- Review papers (acceptable but cannot be the majority)
- Authoritative reference works (textbooks)
- Authoritative websites (NIH, NSF, NASA, etc. — not personal blogs)

Cite in a consistent style (APA, MLA, Vancouver — match the discipline's convention).

## Submission package

The Research Plan accompanies these baseline forms (per `/isef-compliance-walker`):
- Form 1 (Adult Sponsor Checklist)
- Form 1A (Student Checklist) — Research Plan attaches to this
- Form 1B (Approval Form) — completed after SRC review

Conditional forms (per substrate clusters) gate additional pre-approvals.

## Format guidelines

- Length: 3-7 pages typical (varies by complexity; some affiliated fairs cap at 4 pages)
- Font: 11-12pt; readable
- Margins: 1 inch standard
- Page numbers
- Header with student name, project title, school

Always check the **local affiliated fair** for additional formatting requirements — some impose
stricter limits than ISEF central.
