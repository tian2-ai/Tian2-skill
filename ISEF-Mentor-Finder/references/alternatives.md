# Alternatives to cold-emailing for mentorship

If the OpenAlex search returns few good leads, or the student isn't getting responses, here
are alternative paths. Surface 2-3 most-relevant alternatives based on the student's profile.

## Paid programs (high success rate but cost-gated)

### Polygence
- https://www.polygence.org/
- Matches HS students with PhD mentors for one-on-one research projects
- Cost: ~$5k+ per semester (financial aid available)
- Format: weekly 1-hour calls; project takes ~3-6 months
- Outcomes: many students publish their projects; some win at ISEF

### Pioneer Academics
- https://pioneeracademics.com/
- College-level research program; mentors are professors at US universities
- Cost: ~$6k+ per program
- Format: in-person + online; produces a research paper
- Strong reputation for college admissions value

### Lumiere Education
- https://www.lumiere-education.com/
- Similar model to Polygence; PhD mentors
- Cost: $3k+ depending on program

## Free / scholarship-based programs (more selective)

### AAAS Mentoring program
- https://www.aaas.org/programs/aaas-iuse/mentoring
- For underrepresented students in STEM
- Free; competitive admission

### NIH STEP-UP (Short-Term Research Experience for Underrepresented Persons)
- https://www.niddk.nih.gov/research-funding/research-programs/step-up
- HS students for summer research at NIH-affiliated labs
- Free; competitive; very strong on biomedical research

### NSF Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU)
- https://www.nsf.gov/crssprgm/reu/
- Officially for undergrads; some sites accept rising college freshmen / advanced HS
- Free; competitive

### Regeneron Science Talent Search
- https://www.societyforscience.org/regeneron-sts/
- Senior-only; the same Regeneron that sponsors ISEF
- Application is itself a vehicle for mentorship feedback

## University programs (varies by region)

### MIT Research Science Institute (RSI)
- https://www.cee.org/research-science-institute
- 6-week summer; HS juniors; free; extremely selective
- Pairs students with MIT/Harvard researchers

### Stanford SIMR (Stanford Institute for Medical Research)
- Summer biomedical research for HS students
- Cost varies; aid available

### Local university summer programs
- Most large research universities host similar summer programs. Search "[University] high
  school summer research" on Google.

## Community programs (lowest barrier)

### Your high school teacher's network
- Many science teachers have a Rolodex of former students, university contacts, industry
  contacts they don't proactively share. Ask. Specifically: "Do you know anyone who works on
  X that I could email?"
- Higher response rate than cold-emailing (warm introduction increases response rate ~5-10×)

### Your parents' network — even if not academic
- Many parents know someone who knows someone. A friend in software at a tech company can
  introduce you to their research team.
- Not as targeted as academic search, but the warm intro changes everything.

### Local STEM nonprofits and maker spaces
- Many cities have maker spaces, science museums, and STEM nonprofits that connect HS students
  with adult mentors. These are NOT research mentors necessarily, but they're project-feedback
  mentors which is often what students actually need.

### University open-house / research-day events
- Most universities host annual events where students can walk through labs and meet faculty
- Often the LOWEST-effort way to meet researchers — they're expecting visitors

## When to use this skill vs. an alternative

| Situation | Best path |
|---|---|
| Student has 6-12 months and a clear topic | This skill (cold email) + paid program if budget allows |
| Student has 2-3 months | Paid program (Polygence) or warm intro via teacher |
| Student is underrepresented in STEM | AAAS / STEP-UP / scholarship-based programs first |
| Student lives near a major research university | University open-house events + cold email |
| Student wants a project but no specific topic | A summer program (RSI / SIMR / STEP-UP) — they assign topics |
| Student is a senior with results already | Regeneron STS application as the "deliverable" |

Always direct the student to talk to their school counselor about scholarship-based programs
before assuming a paid program is the only option.
