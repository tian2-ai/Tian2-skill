# ISEF Display & Safety Rules — Poster Constraints

**Source:** `/Volumes/Mac-Mini/workspaces/tian2-edu/Competitions/isef-research-playbook/05-analysis/compliance-form-decision-tree-2026-05-01.md` §6, citing ISEF 2026 DS-Rules.pdf.

## Booth specifications

Hard physical limits (verified):

| Dimension | Limit |
|---|---|
| Depth | 76 cm |
| Width | 122 cm |
| Height | 240 cm |

The poster must fit within the booth backplane — practical max poster width ≈ 122 cm minus
mounting margin. The booth height of 240 cm includes the table; practical poster vertical
real estate ≈ 165 cm above table.

## Required vertically-displayed items

These MUST be visible on the booth backplane:

1. **Official Abstract & Certification** — SRC-stamped/embossed version only
2. **ISEF Project Set-up Approval Form** — received on-site, signed by finalist + D&S Committee
3. **Form 1C** — if any RRI / industrial / non-home-school-field-site work
4. **Form 7** — if this is a continuation/progression project

The poster itself is one of these vertically-displayed items; the abstract is a SEPARATE
displayed item (don't bury it inside the poster; the official stamped version must be visible
as its own document).

## Required available items (not necessarily displayed)

Must be at the booth and ready to show on request:

- Form 1 (Adult Sponsor Checklist)
- Form 1A (Student Checklist)
- Research Plan / Project Summary
- Form 1B (Approval Form)
- Photo/video release form (if applicable)
- Lab notebook / project data book (judges may ask)

## Items NOT permitted at the booth

- Completed informed-consent forms for human-participant studies (a BLANK sample may live in the logbook)
- Live organisms
- Hazardous chemicals
- Glass items
- Sharps
- Class 3B or Class 4 lasers (only Class 1, 2, 3A, 3R are allowed)
- Lighting other than LED

## Layout implications

The poster should reserve real estate for:
- The poster proper: your title, abstract preview, methods, results, conclusions, references
- Adjacent: the SRC-stamped abstract document (treat as a separate sheet posted alongside)
- Adjacent: any Form 1C / 7 documents (conditional)
- A QR code linking to extended materials (allowed; many students include one)

Don't try to fit the formal abstract document INTO the poster — it's a separate stamped sheet.
Your poster's "abstract" section can be a tighter overview.

## Judging interview considerations

The poster is what judges read during their 6-minute walk-by AND what they reference during the
15-minute interview. Layout principles:

- Reading order: top-left → top-right → bottom-left → bottom-right (Western Z-pattern)
- Title + author + school visible from 3 meters
- Body text legible from 1 meter
- Figures with self-explanatory captions (judges may read them without your narration)
- "So what?" / impact statement prominent (judges scan for this)

## What this means for layout

| Region | Content |
|---|---|
| Banner (top) | Title, author, school, ISEF category + sub-category, category number (e.g. "PHYS021") |
| Top-left | Brief abstract / introduction / hypothesis |
| Top-right | Methods (compact) |
| Center (largest) | Results: figures with captions |
| Bottom-left | Discussion / interpretation |
| Bottom-right | Significance / impact / future work |
| Footer | References (≥5), acknowledgments (mentor + funder if applicable), AI-use disclosure |

Some category-specific variations exist (MATH posters are often single-column theorem-proof
flow; ROBO posters often have a large prototype photo center-top; CBIO posters often have
multi-figure result grids). Adapt to the category's conventions per
`isef-judging-panel-researcher/references/category-judge-personas.md`.
