# Per-Section Intake Prompts

Use these prompts during Step 2 of the workflow. **One question at a time; capture answers in
the student's own words.**

## §A — Rationale (3-5 prompts)

1. "What's the scientific problem or real-world need your project addresses, in one sentence?"
2. "What's currently *unknown* about this problem? What gap in knowledge will your work narrow?"
   (If the student says "nothing is known" — push back; almost no problem has zero prior work.)
3. "Why does narrowing this gap matter? Who benefits? Be specific — 'cancer patients' is too
   broad; 'people with a specific subtype of triple-negative breast cancer' is specific."
4. "What's the smallest version of progress you could make in your project's time window?"
5. (Optional) "Is your rationale tied to a specific funding initiative, paper, news event, or
   personal connection? Mentioning a real anchor adds credibility."

## §B — Research Question / Hypothesis (4 prompts)

For science projects:

1. "What is your specific research question? State it as a question."
2. "What's your hypothesis — a testable prediction about the answer?"
3. "What experimental or analytical result would *falsify* your hypothesis? (If you can't answer
   this, the hypothesis isn't yet falsifiable — let's sharpen it.)"
4. "What's the dependent variable (what you measure)? What's the independent variable (what you
   manipulate)? What's controlled?"

For engineering projects:

1. "What's the problem you're solving and who is the user?"
2. "What are the design criteria the solution must meet?"
3. "What are the constraints (budget, time, materials, regulatory)?"
4. "How will you measure success? Quantify if possible."

## §C — Methodology / Procedures (5-7 prompts)

1. "Walk me through what you'd do in your first week of experimentation, step by step."
2. "What equipment / materials do you need? Do you have access to all of them?"
3. "What's your sample size? How did you choose it?"
4. "What are your controls? What's your baseline?"
5. "What could go wrong in the methodology? What's your contingency?"
6. "How long does each experimental run take? How many runs in total?"
7. "Have you done a pilot or done this protocol before? If yes, describe; if no, when will you
   pilot it?"

## §D — Risk Assessment (substrate-cluster prompts)

Pull from `/isef-compliance-walker` results if available. For each cluster that applies:

**Cluster A (vertebrate animals):**
- "Which species? How many? Source?"
- "Housing protocol? Vet on call?"
- "Humane endpoint criteria? Euthanasia method (if applicable)?"
- "Form 5A or 5B pre-approved by SRC/IACUC?"

**Cluster B (human participants):**
- "How will you recruit? What's the consent process?"
- "If minors are participants: parental permission?"
- "Privacy: how is data stored? Anonymized when?"
- "IRB-approved consent form ready?"

**Cluster C (tissue/PHBA):**
- "Source and biosafety level?"
- "Disposal protocol?"
- "BSL-1 or BSL-2 checklist completed and attached to Form 6A?"

**Cluster E (hazardous chemicals):**
- "List the chemicals. SDS reviewed?"
- "PPE? Ventilation?"
- "Disposal protocol?"
- "DEA-controlled? Form 2 + Qualified Scientist required."

**Cluster F (low-substrate / computational):**
- Even computational projects have IP, dual-use, or data-privacy considerations. "Does your
  data come from a source with use restrictions (e.g., medical-research datasets with usage
  agreements)?"

## §E — Data Analysis (4 prompts)

1. "Which statistical tests are appropriate for your design? (We can use `/isef-data-analysis-tutor`
   if you're unsure.)"
2. "What's your plan for outliers? Removal criteria stated in advance?"
3. "How will you handle missing data?"
4. "Which analyses are pre-registered (planned in advance) vs. exploratory (looking for
   patterns)? Be explicit — judges and SRCs respect honesty here."

## §F — Bibliography (2-3 prompts)

1. "Which 3-5 primary research papers have you read on this topic? List by author, year."
2. "Which review papers or textbook chapters give you the foundational background?"
3. "If your count is below 5, let's pause and find more (`/literature-review` can help)."

## §G — Modifications log (only if continuation)

For Form 7 continuations:
1. "What's new this year compared to your prior project?"
2. "What was the prior project's main finding?"
3. "Why is this year's work meaningful progression (not just 'more of the same')?"
