# High-School Curriculum Anchors

**Purpose:** Compute T2.2 "high-school curriculum hook" (10 pts). A topic must map to a specific course the student is taking or has taken — otherwise the student can't anchor explanations for teachers, parents, and (non-specialist) judges.

Coverage: AP (US), IB (international), CN-gaokao + 高考竞赛 (China), A-level (UK).

## §1 — Anchor-finding rules

For each topic, identify:
1. The **foundational chapter** in a standard textbook (not just a paper)
2. The **specific course** that covers it (AP/IB/CN/A-level)
3. The **prerequisite** topics within that course

If the topic foundation requires post-AP coursework (e.g., real analysis, graduate biology) but the student is a HS junior, T2.2 ≤ 5. If the foundation maps to a course the student has explicitly taken, T2.2 ≥ 8.

## §3 — Scoring guidance

| Situation | T2.2 score |
|-----------|-----------|
| Topic foundation is in a course the student lists as taking/taken | 9–10/10 |
| Topic foundation is in a course adjacent to one taken (e.g., AP Chem when student has AP Bio) | 6–8/10 |
| Topic foundation is one course beyond what the student has taken (e.g., AP Stats when student is in pre-AP) | 4–6/10 |
| Topic foundation requires undergraduate-level coursework | 2–4/10 |
| Topic foundation requires graduate-level coursework | 1–2/10 |

If the student profile didn't capture coursework explicitly, ask one targeted question in `flow-discover.md` §2 question 2 ("background").

## §4 — Anchor explanation requirement

A high T2.2 score also requires that the student can verbally connect the topic to the course. The rubric estimates this from the student's hypothesis-articulation gate output (`flow-score.md` §3) — if the hypothesis uses terminology not in the foundational textbook chapter, T2.2 is capped at 6/10 regardless of which course they list.

## §5 — China-specific notes

China-track students often have **deeper subject-specific preparation** (高考竞赛 / Olympiad) but **narrower coursework breadth** than US AP students. A Chinese student with deep physics olympiad prep can anchor topics that an AP Physics student cannot — but they may struggle with broad cross-disciplinary anchors.

Adjust accordingly: a Chinese student citing 物理竞赛 background for a PHYS topic deserves T2.2 ≥ 8; the same student attempting a BMED topic without explicit biology coursework should not.
